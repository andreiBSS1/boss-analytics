Q1 2026 Marketing Performance Review

Weekly analysis of paid media efficiency, pipeline conversion, and revenue impact across Facebook, Google Ads, and the Monday.com sales workflow
Period  Jan 1 – Mar 31, 2026 Markets  NV · AZ · CA · TX · MN Sources  Windsor.ai · Monday.com Prepared  March 31, 2026
BSS invested $171K in paid media during Q1, generating 1,606 Facebook leads and 828 Google conversions — but Nevada Facebook CPL reached crisis levels in mid-February ($265–372/lead), while Google PMax emerged as the most scalable growth engine at 4.2× ROAS. The pipeline shows a critical bottleneck: 37% of Q1 leads remain untouched in "Lead" status, representing $200K+ in at-risk revenue.
  • NV Facebook is over-indexed and under-performing. 63% of total spend ($107K) went to Facebook, but NV campaigns averaged $187 CPL — 4× more expensive than California at $37 CPL. The worst two weeks (Feb 9–22) saw CPL exceed $300.
  • Google PMax is the most efficient channel. PMax delivered 533 conversions at $63 CPA with 4.2× ROAS. California PMax achieved $29 CPA — the single best unit economics across all campaigns and platforms.
  • $7K/quarter in YouTube produces near-zero measurable conversions. Five YouTube awareness campaigns generated only 27 attributed conversions across 13 weeks. Without view-through attribution, this spend is unaccountable.
  • Done Done revenue hit $1.14M in Q1 — averaging $10,857 per completed job — with March tracking toward $470K. April is forecasted at $580–650K, potentially clearing the $600K monthly target.
