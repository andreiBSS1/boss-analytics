05Executive Insights Report
Executive Summary — Week of Mar 30 – Apr 5, 2026  •  5-Week Context
This week's $148,610 in revenue at 9.4x ROI is the weakest marketing efficiency result in five weeks, and it sits 16% below the prior week. But the more important story is structural: the Lead→Estimate rate has declined every single week since Mar 2, from 42.4% to 19.2% — a 55% collapse that no single week's revenue figure reflects. The business is generating more leads than ever (255 this week, 4-wk high) while converting a smaller share of them to estimates than at any point this month. Simultaneously, Arizona's ROI has compounded from 9.4x → 21.4x → 34.1x over three consecutive weeks while receiving $1,631 in weekly spend — the lowest budget of any active market.

Marketing spend has increased from $12,854 (W2) to $15,841 (W4) — a 23% increase — while blended ROI has declined from 14.1x to 9.4x. This is not a temporary dip: every week with available data shows compressing returns. Sales Operations
The mid-funnel is broken and has been breaking for four consecutive weeks

At the team's 34.7% close rate, that gap represents approximately 20 additional closed deals and $175K in recoverable revenue — every single week this problem goes unresolved.

Rec 02
Systemic Fix — Highest Priority
Fix the mid-funnel process breakdown — this is the single largest lever in the business
The 4-week L→E decline from 42.4% to 19.2% is the company's most expensive unresolved problem. Every percentage point of recovery is worth approximately $6,800 in weekly revenue at current lead volume and close rates. Returning to W1's 42.4% rate would add ~$175K/week in revenue without spending another dollar on marketing.

Audit Monday.com lead-to-estimate time across all boards. If the bottleneck is speed-to-contact, implement a same-day SLA. If it is routing, assign dedicated inbound reps by state. Pull W1 estimate activity logs and compare rep contact behavior to current week.
Pull Monday.com estimate scheduling data for W1 vs this week. Identify the process change that occurred between Mar 2-8 and Mar 9-15 when the rate first collapsed.
